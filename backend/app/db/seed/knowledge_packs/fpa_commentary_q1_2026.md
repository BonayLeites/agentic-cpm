# FP&A Commentary — Q1 2026 Performance Review

## NKG-SG: Margin Pressure

Singapore's gross margin declined from 48.2% in January to 45.0% in February, driven by two factors: (1) aggressive pricing on two new enterprise deals secured in the APAC market, resulting in a price/mix impact of approximately -2.1 percentage points, and (2) rising delivery costs due to contractor rate increases from our primary staffing partner, contributing an additional -2.1 percentage points of margin erosion. Neither effect is reflected in the current FY2026 forecast. The local team argues the pricing was necessary to establish market presence, but the cost overruns were unexpected. Recommend revising the Singapore forecast and reviewing contractor rate negotiations.

## NKG-US: Client Loss and Cash Collection

The US entity lost one enterprise client (TechVentures Inc.) in February, resulting in a monthly recurring revenue impact of approximately USD 340K. The client cited competitive pricing from a regional provider. This churn event has not yet been reflected in the revised forecast. Additionally, Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) increased from 53 days in January to 58 days in February, primarily driven by delayed payments from three mid-market accounts. The AR team has initiated collection calls but the trend warrants attention at the group level. A group-wide cash collection initiative focused on receivables aged over 60 days is recommended.

## NKG-JP: Solid Performance

Japan continues to outperform budget with revenue 5% above plan and stable margins. The consulting utilization rate improved slightly to 78%, and MRR growth remains on track. No material issues flagged.
