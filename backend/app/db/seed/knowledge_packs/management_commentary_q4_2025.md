# Management Commentary — Q4 2025 Review

## Group Performance

NikkoGroup closed Q4 2025 with consolidated revenue of JPY 3.9B, slightly above the full-year run-rate target. The group operating margin landed at 11.2%, within the guidance range of 10-13%. Cash conversion remained healthy with group DSO at 50 days, in line with the industry benchmark.

## Entity Highlights

**NKG-JP**: Stable quarter with consistent consulting demand from financial services clients. MRR grew 4.2% QoQ, driven by two new enterprise SaaS contracts. Churn remained well-controlled at 1.0%. The team is focused on cross-selling cloud migration services to the existing SaaS client base in FY2026.

**NKG-US**: Revenue growth of 8% YoY but margin pressure from a shift toward smaller, lower-margin deals in the mid-market segment. The sales team added 15 new logos but average deal size decreased 12%. NPS remained solid at 71. Key risk: pipeline is healthy (+35% YoY) but the conversion rate has softened. Management expects the revenue mix to normalize in Q2 2026 as two large enterprise deals close.

**NKG-SG**: First full year of operations completed. Revenue reached SGD 3.4M, slightly below the original plan of SGD 3.6M. Gross margin averaged 47%, reflecting the competitive pricing strategy required for market entry. Key achievement: secured 3 anchor enterprise clients. Key concern: heavy reliance on contractors (65% of delivery staff) drives higher costs. Plan for FY2026: gradually shift to 50% permanent / 50% contractor mix to improve margins by 3-4 percentage points.

## Outlook for FY2026

Group revenue target: JPY 4.2B (+8% YoY). Key initiatives: Singapore margin improvement program, US enterprise focus strategy, Japan cross-sell campaign. Risks: FX volatility (USD/JPY), Singapore contractor cost inflation, US mid-market competition.
